Engineering Thesis

Mobile Vital Signs Monitoring Kit with Web Application

A healthcare communication solution designed for remote areas.

Thesis Project

1.2 Objective of the Study

This study designed a vital sign monitoring system for evacuation centers that allows patients to send their vital signs such as blood pressure, body temperature and pulse rate through a web application. Health professionals can then send diagnoses and medical prescriptions based on the readings.

Specific Objectives:
  • Program sensors in a microcontroller to measure Blood Pressure, Temperature, and Pulse Rate.
  • Design a local and cloud database system to manage patient biometrics and professional records.
  • Create a web application with notification systems for real-time monitoring.
1.3 Significance of the Study

This study benefits citizens (teens, adults, and elders) by providing immediate medical feedback. It assists health professionals in delivering diagnosis via smartphones and acts as a foundation for future healthcare communication research.

1.4 Scope and Limitation

Focused on specific parameters: blood pressure, pulse rate, and temperature. The system features a Hybrid Database: data is stored in a local database when offline and automatically synced to the cloud once internet becomes available.
